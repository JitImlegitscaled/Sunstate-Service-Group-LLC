The Four Systems We Inspect

A 4-point inspection evaluates the four major systems of your home that insurance companies need to assess risk and determine eligibility.

🏠

Roofing System

We assess the age, condition, material type, and remaining useful life of your roof. We document any visible damage, leaks, or areas of concern that insurers need to know about.

⚡

Electrical System

Inspection of the main panel, wiring type (copper, aluminum, knob-and-tube), amp service, breaker conditions, and any safety hazards. Outdated wiring can be a deal-breaker for insurers.

💧

Plumbing System

We evaluate pipe material (copper, PVC, polybutylene, galvanized), water heater age and condition, supply and drain lines, and any visible leaks or water damage throughout the home.

❄️

HVAC System

Assessment of your heating, ventilation, and air conditioning equipment including age, type, condition, and functionality. In Florida's climate, a well-maintained HVAC system is critical.

Why Do You Need a
4-Point Inspection?

Florida insurance companies typically require a 4-point inspection for homes that are 25 years or older. Some insurers may require one for homes as young as 15 years. Without this inspection, you may not be able to obtain or renew your homeowners insurance.
