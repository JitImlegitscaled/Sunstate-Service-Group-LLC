Why at 11 Months?

Most new home builders provide a one-year warranty. By scheduling your inspection at the 11-month mark, you give yourself time to document defects and have the builder address them before your warranty expires.

🆕

Month 0: Move In

You close on your new home and the builder's one-year warranty clock starts ticking.

🔍

Month 11: We Inspect

Our thorough inspection identifies defects, settlement issues, and items that have developed over the first year of living in the home.

✅

Month 12: Builder Fixes

You present the report to your builder. They address the documented issues under warranty — saving you thousands in out-of-pocket repairs.

Common Issues
We Uncover

After living in your home for nearly a year, many defects become apparent that weren't visible on move-in day. Settling, weather exposure, and daily use reveal the true quality of construction.

  • ✓ Drywall cracks from foundation settling
  • ✓ Grading and drainage issues causing moisture intrusion
  • ✓ Improperly sealed windows, doors, and penetrations
  • ✓ HVAC performance issues and duct leaks
  • ✓ Plumbing leaks under sinks and in walls
  • ✓ Stucco cracks, paint failures, and exterior defects
