Construction Phases

Key Inspection Phases

We can inspect your new home at critical stages during construction to catch issues early, when they're easiest and cheapest to fix.

01

Pre-Drywall / Framing

Before walls are closed up, we inspect framing, electrical rough-in, plumbing rough-in, HVAC ductwork, insulation, and structural connections. This is the most critical phase — defects hidden behind drywall are costly to fix later.

Most Critical Phase
02

Pre-Final Walkthrough

Before your final walkthrough with the builder, we do a thorough inspection of the finished home. We check all systems, finishes, fixtures, appliances, and identify any punch-list items the builder needs to address.

Before Builder Walkthrough
03

Final / Closing Inspection

Just before closing, we verify that all punch-list items have been properly completed, all systems are functioning correctly, and the home is ready for move-in. Your last line of defense before taking ownership.

Last Defense Before Closing
Why It Matters

Why New Homes Still
Need Inspections

Many buyers assume a new home is defect-free. In reality, construction defects are common — even in quality builds. Municipal inspectors can't catch everything, and builders have tight schedules that sometimes lead to oversights.

  • ✓ Builders work on tight timelines — mistakes happen
  • ✓ Municipal code inspectors focus on code compliance, not quality
  • ✓ Catch issues while still covered under the builder's warranty
  • ✓ An independent inspector works for YOU, not the builder
